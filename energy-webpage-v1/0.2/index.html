Understanding Energy Consumption in Australia

Australian households use an average of 18-20 kWh of electricity per day. With rising energy costs and environmental concerns, choosing energy-efficient appliances has never been more important.

The Energy Rating Label helps you compare the energy efficiency of appliances. More stars mean better efficiency and lower running costs. A typical Australian household can save hundreds of dollars annually by choosing appliances with higher star ratings.

Why Energy Efficiency Matters

Energy-efficient appliances reduce your electricity bills, lower your carbon footprint, and contribute to a more sustainable future for Australia. With electricity prices continuing to rise, the savings from efficient appliances compound over time.

The Australian government's Energy Rating scheme provides standardized information to help consumers make informed decisions about appliance purchases.

Tips for Reducing TV Energy Consumption

• Turn off your TV when not in use rather than leaving it on standby

• Reduce screen brightness to comfortable levels

• Enable power-saving modes available in most modern TVs

• Consider screen size carefully - larger screens use more energy

• Look for TVs with high energy star ratings when purchasing

Energy Efficiency in Australia

Australia has one of the highest per capita energy consumption rates in the world. However, we also have tremendous solar potential and a growing commitment to renewable energy. By choosing energy-efficient appliances, Australian households can play a crucial role in reducing overall energy demand.

The Australian government's Energy Rating scheme has been instrumental in driving improvements in appliance efficiency, saving households billions of dollars collectively while reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
